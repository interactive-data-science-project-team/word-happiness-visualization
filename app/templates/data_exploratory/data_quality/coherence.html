Coherence

The data is quite coherent. In the table below, we show the summary statistics for each continuous variable. The standard deviation for all the variables is relatively small, which suggest that we have very few outliers in the dataset.

In the pair plot below, we show the scatter plot between six covariates and their respective histogram. The plot shows that the covariates are correlated, for example, log GDB is strongly correlated with life expectancy at birth. This brings challenges for training regression models. Also, The histograms show that the covariates skewed.

The histogram (below) of the happiness score resembles a normal distribution, but has shorter tails.

The plot below (left) shows the top 10 counties with the highest happiness score in 2017. Finland is the highest ranked. The plot on the right shows 10 lowest ranked countries in 2017.

The box plots below shows the box plots of happiness score, GDP, life expectancy, and democratic quality from 2005 to 2017. We see that these variables have a consistent distribution over the years, except in 2005. However, since only 27 countries reported happiness score in 2005, the data may be biased towards higher ranked countries.
