Correctness

The data collected for this analysis mainly comes from two sources, the official statistics provided by international organizations like the World Bank and the World Health Organization, and survey responses from the Gallup World Poll. We can rely on the correctness of the data to some extent given the credibility of these organizations and that the methodologies used to collect data is subject to public review. For example, the methodology used by the World Bank to create the World Development Indicator is described here, and the methodology used for the Gallup World Poll is available here. Also, some attributes are aggregated metrics themselves, such as "Democratic Quality", we can assume they exhibit low variance.
